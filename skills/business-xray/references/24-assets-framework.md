# 24 Assets Framework — Scoring Reference

**Source:** Daniel Priestley, *24 Assets* (Rethink Press, 2017)
**Purpose:** Score each of the 24 assets as Green/Yellow/Red during the Business X-Ray Assessment phase to calculate a Leverage Score and build a prioritized upgrade roadmap.

---

## 1. Overview

Daniel Priestley's 24 Assets framework maps every valuable element a business can own into 7 categories. Unlike revenue or headcount, assets are what a business is *worth* — they generate value without the owner being present.

**The premise:** A business with weak assets is just a job. A business with strong assets is an investment.

**7 Categories:**

| # | Category | Focus |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | Intellectual Property | What the business knows and has created |
| 2 | Brand | What people think and feel about the business |
| 3 | Market | How the business reaches and owns its audience |
| 4 | Product | What the business sells across the buyer journey |
| 5 | Systems | How the business runs without the owner |
| 6 | Culture | Who the business attracts and develops |
| 7 | Funding | How the business is structured for investment |

**The Assessment Goal:** Score all 24 assets, calculate a Leverage Score, and identify which assets to upgrade first based on their category tier (Critical/Important/Secondary) for this specific business.

---

## 2. The 4 Quality Tests

Every asset is tested against these 4 questions. Green = passes all 4. Yellow = passes some. Red = passes none.

| Test | Question | What it Detects |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| **Remarkability Test** | If someone encountered this asset, would they tell a friend about it? | Whether it creates word-of-mouth value |
| **90-Day Yachting Test** | Would this asset keep working if the owner disappeared for 90 days? | Dependency on the owner's presence |
| **Digital Scalability Test** | Can it be emailed or shared digitally and still deliver full value? | Whether it's locked to physical delivery |
| **Evergreen Test** | Will this asset still be relevant and valuable in 3 years? | Whether it's trend-dependent or durable |

**How to apply during scoring:** Run each asset through these tests mentally. If an asset passes 3 out of 4, it's borderline Yellow-Green. If it passes all 4, it's Green. If it fails 3 or more, it's Red.

## 3. Scoring Rubric

| Score | Color | Hex Fill | Hex Stroke | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|----------|
| **Green** | Remarkable | `#d5e8d4` | `#82b366` | Professionally produced. Passes all 4 quality tests. A stranger who encountered it would be impressed. Runs without the owner present. |
| **Yellow** | Basic/Functional | `#fff2cc` | `#d6b656` | Exists and does a job. Owner-made or inconsistent. Wouldn't impress a stranger. Passes 1–2 quality tests. Dependent on owner for delivery or updates. |
| **Red** | Missing | `#f8cecc` | `#b85450` | Does not exist, or exists only in the owner's head. Has no physical or digital presence. Would score zero with an investor or acquirer. |

**Quick scoring heuristic:** "Would an investor who'd never met the owner see this asset and say it has value?" Yes = at least Yellow. "Would they say it's exceptional and defensible?" Yes = Green.

---

## 4. Leverage Score

**Formula:**

```
Active Assets = 24 - Deprioritized count
Leverage Score = (Green assets in Active pool) / Active Assets × 100%
```

Deprioritized assets (gray fill + colored border on scorecard) are excluded from both numerator and denominator. This gives a more honest score — a solo coach who correctly deprioritizes 6 team/funding assets gets scored on 18 assets, not penalized for things that don't apply.

**What it means:**

| Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| 0–30% | Owner-dependent. Revenue stops if they step back. |
| 31–50% | Some leverage. Business runs partly without them. |
| 51–69% | Functional leverage. Can take short breaks without revenue impact. |
| 70%+ | The business runs without the owner. Investable and sellable. |

**How to present the score:**

> "Your Leverage Score is X%. At 70%+, the business runs without you — it's a true asset, not just a job. Right now, your biggest leverage gaps are in [top 2–3 Red/Yellow Critical assets]."

**Deprioritized assets** (gray fill + colored border on the scorecard) are excluded from the Leverage Score entirely. They still get scored Green/Yellow/Red but don't count toward or against the percentage. This gives solo operators and early-stage businesses a fairer score without penalizing them for assets that genuinely don't apply yet.

## 5. Relevance Tiers

Categories are ranked Critical/Important/Secondary based on the business archetype identified during the Business Map phase. These are default baselines — interview data always overrides.

| Category | Coach | Course Creator | Agency | SaaS | Service Provider | Creator |
|----------|-------|----------------|--------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **IP** | Critical | Critical | Important | Critical | Important | Critical |
| **Brand** | Critical | Critical | Important | Important | Important | Critical |
| **Market** | Critical | Critical | Important | Critical | Important | Critical |
| **Product** | Critical | Critical | Important | Critical | Critical | Important |
| **Systems** | Important | Important | Critical | Critical | Critical | Important |
| **Culture** | Secondary | Secondary | Critical | Important | Important | Secondary |
| **Funding** | Secondary | Secondary | Secondary | Critical | Secondary | Secondary |

**Override rules — always adjust when interview data shows:**

| Signal | Adjustment |
|--------|-----------|
| User mentions exit plan, acquisition, or selling the business | Funding → Critical |
| User has or is building a team beyond 3 people | Culture → Important |
| User is raising investment or has investors | Funding → Critical, Structure → Critical |
| User says "I want this to run without me" | Systems → Critical for all archetypes |
| User is solo with no plans to hire | Culture → Secondary for all archetypes |
| User's revenue is 80%+ from one channel | Market → Critical for all archetypes |

## 6. The 24 Assets — Complete Reference

### CATEGORY 1 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSETS

---

### Asset 1: Content

**Category:** Intellectual Property
**What it is:** Libraries of text, video, audio, and images that differentiate the business, drive discoverability, and build sales credibility — the archive that lets people get to know the business at scale.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Professional production quality (video, photography, design). Systematic repurposing workflow exists. 100+ pieces across multiple formats. Gets shared without prompting. Evergreen — not tied to news cycles. | Content exists but is owner-made and inconsistent. No repurposing system. Sporadic posting. Mix of polished and rough-cut. Would not impress a new prospect. | No content library. No blog, video, audio, or published PDF. Relies entirely on word-of-mouth or paid ads. Nothing to send a prospect to get to know the business. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- TRAFFIC column shows 2+ active organic channels (YouTube, podcast, newsletter) → Content is at least Yellow
- TRAFFIC column shows only paid ads or referrals with no organic → Content is Red
- GOALS column includes "publish X per week" type actions → Content is Yellow
- User mentioned professional video production or ghostwriting → Content is Green
- User said "I post sometimes" or "I've been meaning to" → Content is Red-Yellow
- Traffic volume mentioned is high (thousands of views/subscribers) → Content is likely Yellow-Green

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Collate all existing assets into one named folder (1 day) | Hire a video production company for monthly recording session (90 min = dozens of clips) |
| Record 3–5 phone videos answering your most common prospect questions (1 weekend) | Work with a publisher or ghostwriter to turn content into a book or premium report |
| Publish a 5–10 page PDF report that explains your core ideas (1 weekend) | Build a systematic repurposing workflow: video → transcript → blog → infographic → clips |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 1–2 weekends. Yellow→Green: 3–6 months + supplier investment.

**Dependencies:** Content is the raw material for almost every other asset. Feeds Methodology (substance), Channels (distribution), Marketing & Sales Systems (automation), Gifts, and P4P. Weak content makes it impossible to build a strong brand or market presence.

---

### Asset 2: Methodology

**Category:** Intellectual Property
**What it is:** A specific, named, repeatable process for getting clients to an outcome — the "recipe" the business owns that competitors cannot easily copy.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Branded, named framework with designed visual. Training materials allow others to use it without the owner present. Can be expressed as software, scorecard, or licensed programme. A stranger would recognise it as proprietary. | Process exists and is explained verbally but not named or documented. Owner can describe it but it lives in their head. No visual or training material. | No defined process. Every client engagement is custom. Owner improvises delivery. "We just figure it out as we go" — nothing teachable or scalable. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- User referred to a named framework by a specific name ("my 5-step system", "the [Name] Method") → Methodology is at least Yellow
- Core Product column shows a branded programme name → Methodology likely exists at Yellow
- Process Swimlane mapped during X-Ray shows consistent phases across all clients → Methodology is Yellow
- User said "every client is different, I customise everything" → Methodology is Red
- User has a scorecard tool or diagnostic → Methodology is Yellow-Green
- User published a book based on their framework → Methodology is Green

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Whiteboard session: map every repeatable process you use to get a result. Name each one. (1–2 days) | Codify the methodology into a branded, visually designed framework (named, poster, slide deck) |
| Capture 1–3 most powerful processes as a one-page diagram. Test with 3 clients or prospects. | Create training content (video walkthroughs, workbooks) so others can learn it without you |
| Give it a name. Even a working name makes it real and shareable. | Explore whether it can become a scorecard tool, software, or licensed training programme |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 1–2 focused days. Yellow→Green: 2–4 months.

**Dependencies:** Methodology gives Content its substance and Core Product its structure and defensibility. It feeds Positioning (a named framework creates a category), Marketing & Sales Systems (the "reason why" in sales conversations), and attracts Key People of Influence. Built from Content; enables Product.

---

### Asset 3: Registered Intellectual Property

**Category:** Intellectual Property
**What it is:** Legally defensible ownership of business name, logo, slogans, URLs, social handles, patents, and key design elements — the protection layer for all other IP.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Full IP audit completed. Trademark registered in all operating markets. All social handles secured. IP Assignment Agreements with all external suppliers. Ongoing monitoring in place. Patents filed where relevant. | Business name trademarked in primary market. Domains secured. Some social handles claimed. No formal IP assignment process with suppliers. No ongoing monitoring. | No trademark. Domain is basic (.net or missing). Social handles not secured. No IP Assignment Agreements with creators. Built on someone else's trademark unknowingly. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- Business is more than 3 years old with a consistent brand name → Registered IP is probably Yellow (trademark likely filed)
- Business is under 1 year old or recently renamed → Registered IP is probably Red
- User mentioned a lawyer or legal setup → ask which IP elements are protected
- User mentioned working with designers, developers, or writers → IP Assignment Agreements likely absent unless asked about
- SaaS or tech product → Patents/code ownership is Critical — probe specifically

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Register business name as trademark (1–2 weeks + filing fees) | Commission a full IP audit with a specialist IP lawyer |
| Secure brand name on all major social platforms — even unused ones (1 day) | File for patents or additional trademarks on key differentiating elements |
| Sign IP Assignment Agreements with every external supplier who creates for you | Establish ongoing IP monitoring and enforcement process |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 1–2 weeks + fees. Yellow→Green: 2–3 months.

**Dependencies:** Protects Content, Methodology, Identity, and all product names. Without it, all other IP assets are legally vulnerable. Critical before seeking Valuation or investor funding — acquirers will walk if IP isn't clean.

### CATEGORY 2 — BRAND ASSETS

---

### Asset 4: Philosophy

**Category:** Brand
**What it is:** The documented vision, values, and beliefs of the business — the "why" that drives decisions, attracts aligned people, and makes the brand identifiable even without a logo.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Philosophy is documented, designed as visual assets, published on website, embedded in team performance reviews. Short explainer video exists. Strangers who read it would feel either attracted or repelled — clear stance. | Philosophy exists in the owner's head and is mentioned verbally. A basic "About" or "Mission" page exists but it's generic. Values stated but not lived. | No documented philosophy. No clear beliefs or values. Owner hasn't thought about why the business exists beyond making money. Nothing a team member could point to. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- User articulated a strong contrarian view or specific stance during the interview → Philosophy exists at Yellow-Green
- User's content (mentioned in Traffic column) is opinion-led or values-driven → Philosophy is at least Yellow
- User couldn't explain why their business is different from competitors → Philosophy is Red
- User referenced a culture doc, team handbook, or values explicitly → Philosophy is Yellow-Green
- Business is solo with no team → Philosophy may be informal but still scoreable at Yellow if owner has clear beliefs

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Write 3 core beliefs your business holds — things you stand for AND against (1 day) | Design the philosophy as visual assets: posters, slides, handbook section |
| Draft a one-paragraph vision and 3–5 values. Publish on your website About page. | Create a 2–3 minute video explaining what values mean in practice with real examples |
| Share with your team (even if that's just you). Get their input and buy-in. | Integrate into performance reviews — score people on how they embody the values |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 1 focused day. Yellow→Green: 4–6 weeks to embed across touchpoints.

**Dependencies:** Philosophy is the foundation for Identity (visual expression of values), Ambassadors (who embody values), Culture assets, and Positioning (values become market differentiators). Without it, Identity is just decoration.

---

### Asset 5: Identity

**Category:** Brand
**What it is:** The consistent visual, verbal, and behavioural expression of the brand across all touchpoints — logo, colours, fonts, photography style, tone of voice, and how the brand shows up everywhere.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Full brand guidelines book produced by a professional agency. Every output — slides, thumbnails, emails, video — uses the guidelines. Shared asset library accessible to all suppliers. Instantly recognisable across platforms. | Basic brand guidelines exist (logo, 1–2 colours, one font). Some consistency but supplier-dependent and inconsistent at edges. Owner made their own Canva version. | No brand guidelines. Logo varies across platforms. Mixed fonts and colours. Every designer does something different. Brand looks different on website vs social vs proposals. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- User mentioned hiring a designer or agency for branding → Identity is at least Yellow-Green
- CONVERTERS column shows professionally designed landing pages → Identity is likely Yellow-Green
- Content mentioned in Traffic column has inconsistent visual style (phone photos vs professional) → Identity is Yellow
- User said "my brand is just my face/name" with no design investment → Identity is Yellow-Red
- User referred to specific brand colours or fonts by name → Identity is at least Yellow

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Choose one font and one or two colours. Commit to them everywhere. (1 day) | Commission full brand guidelines from an award-winning branding agency |
| Produce a one-page brand guidelines doc with logo variations, colour codes, font names. Distribute to every supplier. | Ensure every output — slides, thumbnails, signatures — uses the guidelines |
| Audit all touchpoints and remove/update off-brand elements | Build a shared brand asset library with approved logos, templates, and palettes |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 1–2 weeks. Yellow→Green: 2–3 months + agency.

**Dependencies:** Identity expresses Philosophy visually. It is the container for all Content (brand look), Products (packaging/design), Marketing & Sales Systems (ad creative), and Culture materials. Inconsistent identity undermines every other asset.

---

### Asset 6: Ambassadors

**Category:** Brand
**What it is:** People who are known, liked, and trusted — clients, celebrities, experts, influencers — whose association transfers credibility and trust to the brand.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Formal ambassador programme with 2–3 aligned individuals with real audience reach. Structured outputs: events, co-created content, regular social mentions. Sponsorship deals creating ongoing content assets. | Testimonials and endorsements exist and are visible. 1–2 micro-influencers or experts have publicly associated with the brand. No formal programme or structured outputs. | No testimonials displayed. No expert endorsements. No influencer or client who actively promotes the business. Results depend entirely on the owner's own reputation. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- User mentioned notable clients or case studies with named companies → Ambassadors is Yellow
- Traffic column shows podcast guest appearances or co-marketing → Ambassadors is Yellow
- User mentioned running events where others speak or appear → Ambassadors is Yellow-Green
- Products column shows a high-ticket programme (implies successful clients) → probe for testimonials
- User said "I don't really have testimonials" or results are private → Ambassadors is Red

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Identify 3–5 people in your network with influence over your audience — ask for a testimonial, endorsement quote, or LinkedIn recommendation (2–4 weeks) | Design a formal ambassador programme: 2–3 aligned individuals, structured outputs |
| Find 1 micro-influencer willing to co-host an event in exchange for value | Sponsor a team, competition, or event for ongoing photo/video assets |
| Collect all existing endorsements and make them visible on your website and sales materials | Run an annual event with ambassadors — creates 12 months of content assets |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 2–4 weeks. Yellow→Green: 3–6 months ongoing.

**Dependencies:** Ambassadors amplify Philosophy (they embody values), boost Positioning (social proof), and fuel Content (photo/video from appearances). They attract Key People of Influence. Requires Identity to be solid so ambassadors have something credible to associate with.

### CATEGORY 3 — MARKET ASSETS

---

### Asset 7: Positioning

**Category:** Market
**What it is:** The defensible, unique advantage the business occupies in the mind of its buyer — being best quality, most affordable, most convenient, most trusted, or owning a specific category.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Named in media. Won recognised industry awards. Holds accreditations. Has a specific category claim no competitor can easily copy. Owns specific Google keywords. A stranger would immediately understand what makes this business different. | Has a positioning statement but it's generic. May have won a smaller award. Is "a good [category]" but not "the only [specific thing]". | Cannot articulate a specific differentiator. Same as competitors. "We're the best" or "quality you can trust" — interchangeable. Nothing that would make a buyer choose them over a direct competitor. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- User mentioned winning awards or accreditations → Positioning is Yellow-Green
- User named a specific niche ("the only [X] for [Y]") → Positioning is Yellow-Green
- User described competing on price or saying "we're cheaper" → Positioning is Yellow-Red
- Methodology is strong and named → Positioning can be Yellow-Green (framework = category)
- User said "I'm in a crowded market" without a clear differentiator → Positioning is Red
- Traffic includes media mentions or press → Positioning is Green

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Define a single positioning statement: "We are the [descriptor] for [specific audience] who want [outcome]." Make it so specific only you can claim it. | Get featured in a high-quality publication or named a preferred supplier by a recognised brand |
| Enter 1–2 relevant industry awards. Win or get shortlisted — "award-winning" is a durable positioning asset. | Build SEO content that owns the Google terms your ideal clients search |
| Join an accreditation or industry association that signals credibility | Document all positioning assets (awards, press logos, accreditations) and display them prominently everywhere |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 2–4 weeks. Yellow→Green: 3–6 months of PR and media strategy.

**Dependencies:** Positioning is strengthened by Content (thought leadership), Ambassadors (credibility transfer), and Methodology (category-defining frameworks). It is the foundation for all Marketing & Sales Systems and directly affects Valuation.

---

### Asset 8: Channels

**Category:** Market
**What it is:** The routes through which products, services, and messages reach customers — both owned channels (built by the business) and earned channels (others who distribute for them).

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Multiple owned channels operating independently: email list 5,000+ engaged subscribers, YouTube with evergreen searchable content, one social platform. Formal channel partnerships with documented commission structures. Regular earned channel appearances (monthly). | One owned channel running with some consistency. Has appeared on other people's platforms but irregularly. No formal channel partnerships. Reaches audience but couldn't survive losing the primary channel. | No owned channel. No email list. No regular publishing. Entirely dependent on one platform they don't own (e.g., only Instagram, only referrals) or entirely dependent on paid ads. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- TRAFFIC column shows 3+ active organic channels → Channels is Yellow-Green
- TRAFFIC column shows only 1 platform → Channels is Yellow (concentration risk)
- TRAFFIC column shows only paid ads or referrals → Channels is Red-Yellow
- User mentioned email list size (any size with active sending) → Channels is at least Yellow
- User mentioned appearing on other people's podcasts or YouTube shows → Channels is Yellow
- User mentioned a formal referral or affiliate partnership → Channels is Yellow-Green

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Build one owned channel to functional level: publish consistently to YouTube, podcast, email list, or blog for 90 days | Build multiple owned channels that run independently |
| Get featured on 2–3 other people's channels (podcasts, YouTube shows, guest posts) — one feature can drive leads for years | Formalise 1–2 channel partnerships with documented commission structures and co-marketing agreements |
| Identify top 3 channel partners (businesses who already sell to your audience) and open a conversation | Systematise earned channel outreach: goal of one appearance per month minimum |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 3–6 months consistent effort. Yellow→Green: 12–24 months.

**Dependencies:** Channels distribute Content and Gifts. They are the delivery mechanism for Marketing & Sales Systems. Strong channels reduce the cost of launching new Products. Data flows back from channels — more owned channels = richer Data asset.

### Asset 9: Data

**Category:** Market
**What it is:** Structured information the business holds about customers and prospects — preferences, behaviours, frustrations, and desires — that enables personalised relationships at scale.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| CRM with rich profiles on all prospects and clients. Scorecard or diagnostic tool collecting data-rich insights at scale. Data dashboard tracking client outcomes. Segmented database with personalised follow-up sequences. Data used to quantify impact and inform product decisions. | CRM or spreadsheet exists with basic contact info. Some notes on clients. Not consistently used. Cannot segment or personalise at scale. Doesn't know what's in their list beyond names and emails. | No CRM. Contacts scattered across email, phone, and notebooks. Cannot identify or contact past clients systematically. No record of who bought what or when. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- User mentioned a named CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive, etc.) → Data is at least Yellow
- FUNNEL column shows a scorecard or diagnostic → Data is Yellow-Green
- User mentioned a newsletter list with active sending → Data is Yellow
- User said "I should track this but I don't" or "I have a spreadsheet somewhere" → Data is Red-Yellow
- TEAM column shows solo operation → Data is likely Yellow at best (no one maintaining it)
- User mentioned knowing client outcomes or tracking results systematically → Data is Green

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Choose a CRM and migrate all contacts (even a spreadsheet is a start) | Build or deploy a scorecard/diagnostic tool that collects data-rich insights while giving value back |
| For top 50 clients, research their profiles: where they live, what they love, what frustrates them | Create a data dashboard tracking key client outcomes and quantifiable impact |
| Add 10 qualifying questions to your lead intake process to gather richer data from prospects | Use data to personalise follow-up sequences: segment database by profile type |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 2–4 weeks setup. Yellow→Green: 3–6 months to build and populate.

**Dependencies:** Data powers Marketing & Sales Systems (targeting, personalisation), improves Core Product delivery, and strengthens Positioning (you can demonstrate quantifiable impact). Data is also a Funding asset — showing client outcome data improves the Business Plan.

### CATEGORY 4 — PRODUCT ASSETS

---

### Asset 10: Gifts

**Category:** Product
**What it is:** Free products given with no strings attached — not even an email address required. Primarily digital. Designed to capture attention, build goodwill, and demonstrate the problem the business solves.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| 3–5 professionally produced gifts in multiple formats (video, PDF, tool, audio) targeting different buyer awareness stages. Has a gift distribution system: automatically shared via social, email, and sales conversations. Gets shared organically. | 1–2 gifts exist and are distributed manually. Owner-produced quality. Loosely distributed. Not systematically given to every prospect. | No free resources. Prospects are sent straight to a sales page or paid offer. Nothing to give away that demonstrates value before purchase. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- CONVERTERS column mentions a lead magnet or free resource → Gifts is at least Yellow
- FUNNELS column shows a webinar or free training → Gifts is Yellow
- User mentioned a free PDF, checklist, or guide they give away → Gifts is Yellow
- Traffic to Gifts ratio is low (lots of traffic but no free offer) → Gifts is Red
- User mentioned a book they've published → Gifts is Green (book = premium gift)
- Nothing in CONVERTERS except direct product pages → Gifts is Red

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Create one downloadable resource (PDF report, audio interview, mini video series) addressing your audience's biggest question — no opt-in required | Create 3–5 gifts in different formats targeting different stages of buyer awareness |
| Give it away across all channels: emails, events, social media, sales conversations | Invest in professional production — a well-produced report, high-profile podcast, or problem-solving tool |
| Measure whether it gets shared and whether it leads to conversations | Build a gift distribution system: gifts automatically shared via social, email sequences, and sales conversations |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 1–2 weeks. Yellow→Green: 1–3 months per gift, ongoing.

**Dependencies:** Gifts are the entry point to the product ecosystem — they feed P4P. They depend on Content (source material) and Channels (distribution). Strong Gifts reduce Marketing & Sales System costs. A remarkable Gift is a brand-building asset in its own right.

---

### Asset 11: Product-for-Prospects (P4P)

**Category:** Product
**What it is:** The first low-commitment purchase — builds trust, offers a quick win, and sets up the conversation for the Core Product. Usually a small investment of time, money, or data.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Green (Remarkable) | Yellow (Basic) | Red (Missing) |
|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Named, fully productised P4P with a brochure/landing page, standardised delivery process, clear outcome, and automated bridge to the Core Product. Gets testimonials. Can run without the owner. | An entry-level offer exists (workshop, session, e-book, webinar) but it's not packaged or named. Described differently to different people. No documented bridge to Core Product. | No entry-level product. Prospects go straight from free content to a high-ticket offer with no intermediate step. High drop-off at the sale stage. |

**Inference Signals (from X-Ray interview data):**

- PRODUCTS column includes a low-ticket offer (under $500) separate from the main product → P4P is at least Yellow
- FUNNELS column shows a webinar, workshop, or strategy session → P4P may exist at Yellow
- User described a "free strategy call" that converts to high-ticket → P4P is Yellow (call = P4P)
- User's only product is high-ticket with nothing below it → P4P is Red
- User mentioned a book, course, or digital product alongside coaching → P4P is Yellow-Green
- User said prospects "just reach out" with no structured first step → P4P is Red

**Action Playbook:**

| Red → Yellow | Yellow → Green |
|---|---|
| Define a clear, named entry-level offer: a workshop, diagnostic session, audit, webinar, e-book — priced to remove risk | Package the P4P as a fully productised experience with a name, clear outcome, and standardised delivery |
| Create a landing page or one-page description: what it is, who it's for, what they get | Build a P4P delivery system: automated webinar, self-serve tool, or scalable group format |
| Deliver consistently and gather testimonials from early participants | Create a clear bridge from P4P to Core Product: a follow-up sequence or natural next step |

**Effort:** Red→Yellow: 2–4 weeks. Yellow→Green: 1–3 months to fully productise.

**Dependencies:** P4P is downstream of Gifts (attention) and upstream of Core Product (revenue). It is the primary input to Marketing & Sales Systems. P4P quality directly affects sales conversion rates and Core Product pipeline.

---

### Asset 12: Core Product

**Category:** Product
**What it is:** The main revenue-generating product — what the business is known for. Solves the client's problem fully. Should be the best in the world for a specific, defined market.

**Scoring Criteria:**

## 7. Dependency Map

The build order for a new or early-stage business. Follow this sequence to avoid building on unstable foundations.

```
FOUNDATION (build first):
  Philosophy → Identity → Registered IP

DIFFERENTIATION (build second):
  Content → Methodology → Positioning → Channels

PRODUCT ECOSYSTEM (build third):
  Gifts → P4P → Core Product → P4C

INFRASTRUCTURE (build alongside products):
  Marketing & Sales Systems → Operations Systems → Mgmt & Admin Systems

TEAM (build as you grow):
  Key People of Influence → Sales & Marketing People → Mgmt & Admin People → Technicians

FUNDING (build when pursuing capital):
  Business Plan → Valuation → Structure → Risk Mitigation

MARKET AMPLIFIERS (ongoing):
  Data → Ambassadors
```

**High-leverage "keystone" assets** — upgrading these unlocks the most other assets:

| Keystone Asset | Why It Matters |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Content** | Raw material for almost everything — Methodology, Gifts, Channels, Brand |
| **Methodology** | Differentiation engine that flows into Product, Sales, and IP simultaneously |
| **Core Product** | Centre of the product ecosystem — everything leads to and extends from it |
| **Marketing & Sales Systems** | Integration layer that connects all IP and Brand assets to revenue |
| **Philosophy** | Foundation of all Culture and Brand assets — without it, Identity is decoration |

**Critical dependency chains to watch:**

- No Methodology → Core Product will always be bespoke and unscalable
- No Channels → Gifts and P4P have no distribution and Marketing Systems are starved
- No Data → Marketing & Sales Systems cannot personalise or optimise
- No Core Product → P4P has nowhere to bridge to; P4C has nothing to extend
- No Structure → Funding assets cannot be activated regardless of quality

## 8. Revenue Unlock by Asset Type

Which assets are load-bearing for revenue per archetype. Upgrading these categories has the highest revenue impact.

| Asset Category | Primary Revenue Mechanism | Revenue Impact on Upgrade |
|----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Methodology** | Creates category ownership; commands premium pricing | Typical: 30–100% price increase when framework is named and branded |
| **Core Product** | Named, productised offer converts better and holds price | Typical: 20–50% revenue increase from reduced discounting and better close rates |
| **Channels** | Owned distribution means lower CAC and more consistent lead flow | Typical: 2–5x lead volume when a second owned channel reaches scale |
| **Marketing & Sales Systems** | Automation compounds content effort into consistent lead flow | Typical: Enables 2–3x revenue without adding headcount |
| **P4C** | Recurring revenue from existing clients; highest margin product | Typical: Adding one P4C to a coaching business adds 20–40% to revenue within 90 days |
| **Positioning** | Clear differentiation reduces price sensitivity and sales cycle | Typical: 20–40% reduction in sales cycle length when position is clear |
| **Data** | Enables personalisation and re-engagement of dormant contacts | Typical: 10–30% revenue recovery from re-engagement campaigns to existing list |
| **Operations Systems** | Reduces owner time in delivery; enables more clients without burnout | Indirect: Creates capacity for 2–3x more clients at same effort level |

**Revenue impact by archetype (which categories move the needle most):**

| Archetype | Highest Revenue Leverage | Why |
|-----------|--------------------------|-----|
| **Coach** | Methodology + Core Product + P4C | Framework justifies premium price; recurring keeps clients longer |
| **Course Creator** | Channels + M&S Systems + Positioning | Distribution scale is the bottleneck; positioning reduces refunds |
| **Agency** | Systems + Technicians + Core Product | Operations quality determines how many clients you can serve |
| **SaaS** | Product + M&S Systems + Data | Product-market fit is table stakes; acquisition and retention are the levers |
| **Service Provider** | Positioning + P4C + Operations Systems | Differentiation wins the proposal; recurring extends lifetime value |
| **Creator** | Channels + Products + Ambassadors | Audience scale and product ecosystem determine monetisation ceiling |
