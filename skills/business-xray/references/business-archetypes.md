# Business Archetypes

**Pre-built patterns for fast inference during interview.**

Use these to quickly guess business structure, then confirm with user.

---

## Column Definitions (IMPORTANT)

| Column | What It Means | Examples |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **TRAFFIC** | Where people discover you | YouTube, LinkedIn, Podcast, SEO |
| **CONVERTERS** | The entry points where traffic lands — the pages/links people click to enter your world | Substack home page, lead magnet landing page, LemonSqueezy product page, free trial signup page |
| **PRODUCTS** | What you sell and at what price | Coaching ($5K), Course ($497), SaaS ($49/mo) |
| **FUNNELS** | The digital selling asset used to sell each product — what someone sees right before they buy | VSL + offer doc, Gamma doc, webinar replay, sales call booking page, checkout page |
| **MATH** | Revenue numbers, pricing, volume | $5K/client x 4 clients = $20K/mo |
| **TEAM** | Who's involved | Solo, VA, contractor |
| **GOALS** | Key activities to keep the business running | Content creation, sales calls, delivery |

**CONVERTERS are NOT lead magnets.** Lead magnets are a type of content. Converters are the PAGES where traffic lands.
**FUNNELS are NOT email sequences.** Funnels are the SELLING ASSETS — the specific page, video, or document that closes the sale.

---

## How to Use

1. Identify archetype from user's initial description
2. Present pre-filled guess based on archetype
3. User confirms or adjusts
4. Proceed with archetype-informed questions

## Archetype 1: Coach / Consultant

### Typical Profile
- **Business Model:** 1-on-1 or group coaching, consulting services
- **Price Range:** $1,500 - $25,000+
- **Team:** Solo or with 1-2 VAs
- **Client Capacity:** 5-20 active clients

### Default Business Map

| Column | Typical Content |
|--------|----------------|
| **TRAFFIC** | YouTube, LinkedIn, Podcast, Referrals |
| **CONVERTERS** | Webinar registration page, Lead magnet landing page, Free assessment page |
| **PRODUCTS** | Discovery call → High-ticket program |
| **FUNNELS** | Webinar replay + offer doc, Sales call booking page, Application page |
| **MATH** | $5K-$25K per client, 2-4 new clients/month |
| **TEAM** | Owner + Admin VA |
| **GOALS** | Scale without losing quality, systematize delivery |

### Default Bow-Tie

- **Awareness:** YouTube videos, LinkedIn posts
- **Nurturing:** Free training, Email list
- **Consideration:** Application form, Discovery call
- **Commit:** Enrollment call, Payment
- **Onboarding:** Intake form, Goals call, Community access
- **Launch:** First coaching session
- **Results:** Transformation, Testimonial, Referral

### Common Pain Points
- Content creation bottleneck (takes too long)
- Discovery calls eating up time
- Onboarding manual and inconsistent
- Follow-up slipping through cracks

## Archetype 2: Course Creator

### Typical Profile
- **Business Model:** Digital courses, memberships, communities
- **Price Range:** $47 - $2,000 for courses, $19-$99/month for memberships
- **Team:** Solo or with 1-2 VAs + contractors
- **Client Capacity:** 10-1000+ students

### Default Business Map

| Column | Typical Content |
|--------|----------------|
| **TRAFFIC** | YouTube, Instagram, Podcasts, Affiliates |
| **CONVERTERS** | Free course landing page, Webinar registration page, Lead magnet page |
| **PRODUCTS** | Low-ticket ($47-$297) → Flagship ($997-$2K) → Community |
| **FUNNELS** | VSL + checkout page, Webinar replay + offer doc, Sales page |
| **MATH** | High volume, lower touch |
| **TEAM** | Owner + Content VA + Tech support |
| **GOALS** | Evergreen revenue, reduce launch stress |

### Default Bow-Tie

- **Awareness:** YouTube tutorials, Social content
- **Nurturing:** Free mini-course, Email list
- **Consideration:** Webinar, Sales page
- **Commit:** Checkout, Payment plan
- **Onboarding:** Welcome sequence, Course access, Community invite
- **Launch:** First module completion
- **Results:** Course completion, Success stories, Community engagement

### Common Pain Points
- Content creation bottleneck
- Launch burnout (prefer evergreen)
- Low completion rates
- Community management time

## Archetype 3: Agency

### Typical Profile
- **Business Model:** Done-for-you services (marketing, design, development)
- **Price Range:** $2,000 - $20,000/month retainers
- **Team:** Owner + specialists + project manager
- **Client Capacity:** 5-15 active clients

### Default Business Map

| Column | Typical Content |
|--------|----------------|
| **TRAFFIC** | Referrals, LinkedIn, Case studies, Networking |
| **CONVERTERS** | Portfolio website, Case study page, Free audit landing page |
| **PRODUCTS** | Project-based ($5K-$50K) or Retainer ($3K-$15K/mo) |
| **FUNNELS** | Proposal doc, SOW/contract page, Calendly booking page |
| **MATH** | Fewer clients, higher value |
| **TEAM** | Owner + PM + Specialists |
| **GOALS** | Predictable revenue, reduce owner dependency |

### Default Bow-Tie

- **Awareness:** Referrals, LinkedIn, Speaking
- **Nurturing:** Case studies, Newsletter
- **Consideration:** Discovery call, Audit
- **Commit:** Proposal, Contract signed
- **Onboarding:** Kickoff call, Access setup, Process orientation
- **Launch:** First deliverable
- **Results:** Ongoing delivery, Results tracking, Referral request

### Common Pain Points
- Owner doing too much client work
- Proposals taking too long
- Scope creep
- Client communication overhead

## Archetype 4: SaaS / Software

### Typical Profile
- **Business Model:** Software subscription
- **Price Range:** $29 - $299/month
- **Team:** Owner + developers + support
- **Client Capacity:** 100-10,000+ users

### Default Business Map

| Column | Typical Content |
|--------|----------------|
| **TRAFFIC** | Content marketing, SEO, Integrations, AppSumo |
| **CONVERTERS** | Free trial signup page, Demo booking page, Freemium landing page |
| **PRODUCTS** | Free → Starter → Pro → Enterprise |
| **FUNNELS** | In-app upgrade page, Pricing page, Demo + proposal doc |
| **MATH** | MRR focus, churn metrics |
| **TEAM** | Owner + Dev + Support + Marketing |
| **GOALS** | Reduce churn, increase LTV, automate support |

### Default Bow-Tie

- **Awareness:** Blog content, SEO, Product Hunt
- **Nurturing:** Free tier, Email onboarding
- **Consideration:** Feature comparison, Demo call (enterprise)
- **Commit:** Upgrade to paid, Annual plan
- **Onboarding:** Product tour, Setup wizard, First use case
- **Launch:** First "aha" moment, Key feature used
- **Results:** Ongoing usage, Expansion, Referral program

### Common Pain Points
- Support tickets overwhelming
- Churn too high
- Onboarding drop-off
- Feature requests backlog

| **MATH** | Time-based billing, capacity limited |
| **TEAM** | Owner + subcontractors |
| **GOALS** | Raise prices, systematize delivery, reduce hours |

### Default Bow-Tie

- **Awareness:** Referrals, LinkedIn
- **Nurturing:** Email, Relationship building
- **Consideration:** Discovery call
- **Commit:** Proposal accepted
- **Onboarding:** Intake, Access setup
- **Launch:** First deliverable
- **Results:** Ongoing service, Testimonial, Referral

- Scope creep
- No systems, everything in head
- Capacity ceiling

## 24 Assets Baseline Relevance Tiers

**Starting points only — interview data always overrides.** If the user mentions exit plans, investors, or scaling beyond solo, upgrade Funding and Culture to Important or Critical.

### Tier Definitions

| Tier | Meaning | Scorecard Styling |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| **Critical** | Directly drives revenue for this business type | Normal colors, prominent |
| **Important** | Matters for growth, affects other Critical assets | Normal colors |
| **Secondary** | Only relevant at scale or for exit | Muted styling (gray header, light boxes) |

### By Archetype

| Category | Coach/Consultant | Course Creator | Agency | SaaS | Service Provider | Creator |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|--------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **IP** | Critical | Critical | Important | Critical | Important | Critical |
| **Brand** | Critical | Critical | Important | Important | Important | Critical |
| **Market** | Critical | Critical | Critical | Critical | Important | Critical |
| **Product** | Critical | Critical | Important | Critical | Important | Critical |
| **Systems** | Important | Important | Critical | Critical | Important | Important |
| **Culture** | Secondary | Secondary | Critical | Critical | Secondary | Secondary |
| **Funding** | Secondary | Secondary | Important | Critical | Secondary | Secondary |

### Override Rules (Interview-Driven)

| Signal from Interview | Override |
|----------------------|----------|
| Mentions hiring, team scaling, building a team | Culture → Important or Critical |
| Mentions exit plans, selling the business, investors | Funding → Critical |
| Solo with no plans to hire | Culture stays Secondary |
| Strong brand presence (large following, recognition) | Brand → Critical |
| Multiple products with complex pricing | Product → Critical |
| Heavy reliance on manual processes | Systems → Critical |
| Revenue past $500K/year | Funding → Important (business planning matters) |

### Reasoning Notes

- **Coach/Consultant:** IP and Brand are Critical because the business IS the person — methodology, content, and personal brand drive everything. Systems are Important (can't scale coaching without them) but Culture is Secondary for solo operators.
- **Course Creator:** Similar to Coach but Product is even more Critical — the course IS the business. IP matters for protecting the course content and methodology.
- **Agency:** Culture and Systems are Critical because the business depends on team execution. IP is less central — it's about delivery quality, not personal brand.
- **SaaS:** Everything matters. Systems and Product are obvious, but Funding is Critical because software requires capital for development and scaling.
- **Service Provider:** Market and Product are Important (not Critical) because referrals drive most business, reducing the need for marketing assets. Culture is Secondary for solo operators.
- **Creator:** Brand and Market are Critical — the audience IS the business. Product becomes Critical as they monetize. Culture is Secondary until they build a team.
